Oh Uganda, Land of Beauty" is the national anthem of Uganda. George Wilberforce Kakoma composed the music and authored the lyrics. It was adopted as the national anthem in 1962, when the country gained independence from the United Kingdom. It is musically one of the shortest national anthems in the world. Consequently, multiple verses are sung when it is performed in public. The anthem consists of three verses, with all often played to honor the nation's independence and identity. George Kakoma, a teacher and musician, created the anthem to reflec the country's beauty and its status as the "Pearl of Africa".
